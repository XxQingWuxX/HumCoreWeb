Source 1: The Epic of Gligamesh

The Epic of Gilgamesh is a poetry about the adventures of a King of Mesopotamia. It have traces all the way to the 1500 BCE but a version, which was known today was written on twelve clay tablets by a Mesopotamian scholar named Sin-liqe-unninni during the 1200 to 1000 BCE. There are translated versions of this that can be found in the UCI library, I need to find the version I need so I don’t know if the source I need is in other UC campus or here in UCI. A few topics this source could be on will be Gilgamesh, Enkidu and Ishtar. Gilgamesh interacts with Enkidu which was made from clay, so Enkidu relates to the topic of nature entanglement and matter, and morns for his death that caused Gilgamesh to set on a journey to revive him. During this journey it’s human vs nature and human vs self as he grows and accept the death of Enkidu. The reason for the topic of Ishtar would be due to the winter lectures on matter where the professor introduced Venus. In their powers and roles, Venus and Ishtar have a lot in common.
